Queue

  • enqueue() - add to rear
  • dequeue() - remove from front
  • peak() - see whats at front
  • isFull() / isNull() - check if full or empty
A queue is like waiting in line (FI-FO). It follows the rule that the first one to join is the first to leave. It's often used in computer science for tasks like scheduling and managing things in order. Imagine people waiting in line to buy ticketsâ€”the first person to arrive gets served first.

A queue's space complexity can vary depending on how it is implemented. In OOP it can have a space complexity of o(n) but using an array with a static size, it has a space complexity of o(1). However because you only ever interact with one item from the structure at a time, the time complexity is always o(1) regardless of the method.
